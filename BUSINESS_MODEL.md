# Cognita Institutional Business Model

## Positioning

Cognita Institute of Artificial Intelligence is a private professional training institute offering non-degree AI learning and skills-development services.

Cognita must not describe itself as degree-granting, credit-bearing, CHED-recognized, TESDA-registered, or otherwise accredited unless the relevant authority has been formally obtained and the claim is accurate at the time it is published.

## Four program pathways

### 1. Cognita Open Learning

**Purpose:** Flexible self-paced education.

**Best for:** Independent learners, career shifters, educators, and professionals who want structured AI learning without a fixed cohort schedule.

**Core products:**

- Self-paced courses
- Topic-based learning collections
- Templates, worksheets, and practical exercises
- Learning Resource Center access
- Optional assessment pathway when offered

**Revenue model:** One-time course purchase, learning bundles, or recurring membership.

**Credential rule:** Buying or completing self-paced content does not automatically include a Certificate of Completion. Assessment and credentialing must be expressly included or purchased separately.

**Current status:** Phased expansion. The website uses an early-access list until the self-paced delivery and access-control systems are ready.

### 2. Cognita Professional Programs

**Purpose:** Guided, facilitator-led professional training.

**Flagship offering:** 10-Week Professional AI Program.

**Best for:** Learners who need accountability, structured deadlines, facilitator feedback, revision guidance, portfolio development, and a formal completion review.

**Core features:**

- Application-based admission
- Fixed cohort and schedule
- Progressive weekly access
- Required practical outputs
- Facilitator review and revision guidance
- Portfolio development
- Capstone and final review
- Certificate review after requirements are satisfied

**Revenue model:** Premium cohort tuition, installment plans, scholarships or approved discounts, and sponsored seats.

**Credential rule:** The Certificate of Completion is included as an eligibility outcome, not an automatic purchase benefit. Attendance alone is insufficient.

**Current status:** Existing Cognita curriculum and student workflow. This is the operational flagship program.

### 3. Cognita Assessment and Credentialing

**Purpose:** Independent evaluation and verification of learning evidence.

**Best for:** Cognita learners requiring completion review, self-paced learners seeking an optional assessment pathway, professionals with eligible portfolio evidence, and institutions requiring verifiable records.

**Core services:**

- Portfolio assessment
- Output and competency review
- Completion decision
- Verified training record
- Certificate issuance when requirements are met
- Public serial-number verification
- Credential revocation and record correction where justified

**Revenue model:** Assessment fee, portfolio-review fee, credentialing package, or inclusion in a Professional Program or Institutional Training agreement.

**Credential rule:** Credentials are issued only after documented requirements and evidence have been reviewed. Cognita should state the exact credential type and must not call a non-degree Certificate of Completion a diploma, degree, or academic transcript.

**Current status:** Certificate review and public verification are present in the existing app. Standalone paid assessment requires additional policy, pricing, reviewer workflow, and eligibility rules before public launch.

### 4. Cognita Institutional Training

**Purpose:** Group and customized AI training for organizations.

**Best for:** Schools, companies, NGOs, LGUs, associations, workforce-development partners, and professional teams.

**Core formats:**

- Open Learning group access
- Guided 10-week institutional cohort
- Short AI literacy workshops
- Educator development
- Workforce productivity training
- Specialized creative, administrative, or entrepreneurship training
- Custom program design
- Optional assessment and credentialing

**Revenue model:** Per-participant fee, fixed project fee, institutional license, annual learning agreement, facilitator fee, customization fee, and assessment package.

**Credential rule:** The proposal and agreement must state whether assessment, training records, and Certificates of Completion are included.

**Current status:** Inquiry and proposal workflow exists. Final delivery is scoped by consultation.

## Why the divisions are separate

The separation protects both the learner and the institution:

- Self-paced access is not misrepresented as high-touch coaching.
- Guided training can command premium pricing because human support is explicit.
- Assessment can remain independent from course sales.
- Institutional clients can receive custom delivery without altering the individual learner offer.
- Cognita can scale Open Learning while retaining Professional Programs as its premium flagship.

## Primary customer journeys

### Independent learner

1. Explore Cognita Open Learning.
2. Purchase or access a self-paced course when available.
3. Complete lessons and practical exercises.
4. Choose whether to apply for an eligible assessment service.
5. Receive a verified record only after meeting the assessment requirements.

### Guided cohort learner

1. Review the 10-Week Professional AI Program.
2. Submit an application.
3. Receive an admissions decision.
4. Complete enrollment, agreement, and payment requirements.
5. Join a cohort and submit weekly outputs.
6. Receive facilitator feedback and revise when necessary.
7. Complete the capstone and portfolio.
8. Undergo final completion review.
9. Receive an approved Certificate of Completion and verifiable record.

### Institutional client

1. Submit an Institutional Training inquiry.
2. Participate in a discovery and scoping process.
3. Receive a proposal defining audience, objectives, schedule, delivery, pricing, assessment, and reporting.
4. Sign the agreement and complete payment requirements.
5. Enroll participants and deliver the program.
6. Receive completion reporting and any contracted credentials.

## Product and system implications

### Existing systems that remain in use

- Applications
- Students
- Batches
- Tracks
- Lessons
- Quizzes
- Submissions
- Facilitator review
- Certificates
- Payments, invoices, receipts, and refunds
- Announcements and messages
- Partner inquiries
- Waitlist and lead records

### Systems needed for later phases

- Open Learning product catalogue
- Course purchase and entitlement rules
- Membership billing, if approved
- Library resource management
- Standalone assessment applications
- Assessment eligibility and reviewer assignment
- Institutional organization accounts
- Group enrollment and reporting
- Program-specific agreements and pricing

## Implementation rule

Cognita should evolve through the existing application and data model. New systems should be added as modules and entities only when their operational requirements are approved. Do not replace the current app with a separate website merely to introduce the new business structure.
