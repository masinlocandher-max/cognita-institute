# Cognita Editorial Image Guidelines

## Purpose

Cognita imagery should make the institute feel credible, premium, human, and technologically current. The photographs support the institutional story and must not make the platform look like a game, a fictional space academy, or an AI-generated brochure.

## Required visual direction

- Photorealistic professional education and workplace photography
- Realistic skin texture, hands, posture, lighting, and environmental details
- Premium contemporary interiors with warm wood, charcoal, navy, and restrained cyan accents
- Silver MacBook laptops for learner, facilitator, reviewer, and institutional-training scenes
- Calm editorial composition inspired by high-end technology campaigns
- Genuine learning, review, collaboration, and professional-work moments
- Inclusive adult learners and professionals
- Sufficient negative space when website text will be placed beside the photograph

## Futuristic treatment

The futuristic identity should come primarily from interface transitions, subtle ambient cyan light, restrained reflections, and clean technology details.

Avoid turning the photographs into cyberpunk scenes. Do not use excessive neon, floating holograms, fantasy planets, cartoon galaxies, or game-like effects.

## Prohibited image content

- Fake certificates, diplomas, transcripts, or accreditation seals
- Readable invented institutional documents
- Fake website interfaces embedded inside photographs
- Placeholder or garbled AI-generated text
- Browser screenshots presented as editorial photography
- Gaming laptops or visibly mismatched devices
- Distorted hands, extra fingers, unnatural faces, or plastic skin
- Generic graduation-cap stock photography as the primary visual language
- Childish space costumes or science-fiction uniforms

## Current website image roles

- Homepage hero: professional learner using a silver MacBook in a premium learning environment
- Cognita Open Learning: independent self-paced study with a silver MacBook
- Cognita Professional Programs: facilitator-led adult cohort with silver MacBooks
- Cognita Assessment and Credentialing: professional reviewer evaluating evidence with a silver MacBook
- Cognita Institutional Training: corporate workshop with participants using silver MacBooks

## Logo treatment in photography

Do not bake the Cognita logo into generated photographs. The website should render the official logo separately in HTML or SVG so it stays sharp, accessible, editable, and consistent at every screen size.

## Operational note

Generated images must be visually reviewed before publication. Any image containing fake text, a fake credential, an inaccurate logo, or implausible anatomy must be rejected or corrected before integration.
